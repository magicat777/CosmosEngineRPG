Chapter 12: Creating Adventures

Design compelling cosmic adventures that leverage the Universal Tag System to create emergent storytelling and memorable experiences.

12.1 Tag-Based Adventure Design

Great Cosmos Engine adventures start with tags that suggest conflict, opportunity, and narrative hooks.

Adventure Tags

Scope Tags

  • [Personal]: Individual stakes
  • [Local]: Community impact
  • [System]: Multi-planet scope
  • [Galactic]: Sector-wide consequences

Conflict Tags

  • [Corporate]: Business interests
  • [Military]: Armed conflict
  • [Mystery]: Unknown threats
  • [Ancient]: Old secrets emerging

Mood Tags

  • [Urgent]: Time pressure
  • [Deadly]: High stakes
  • [Mysterious]: Hidden elements
  • [Epic]: Larger-than-life

12.2 Adventure Structure

Three-Act Structure

Act 1: Hook and escalation - introduce tags and stakes

Act 2: Complications - tag conflicts and synergies

Act 3: Resolution - tag-driven climax

Tag Spotlight Rotation

Ensure every PC gets moments where their tags are essential to success.

Design multiple solution paths using different tag combinations.

12.3 Example Adventure: The Nexus Station Crisis

Adventure Tags: [Station], [Urgent], [Mysterious], [Deadly]

The Hook

Nexus Station, a critical trade hub, has gone silent. Last transmission mentioned "the shadows are moving." The PCs are the closest ship.

Tag Opportunities

  • [Pilot] tags: Navigate debris field around station
  • [Technical] tags: Restore station systems
  • [Combat] tags: Fight shadow creatures
  • [Social] tags: Calm panicked survivors
  • [Knowledge] tags: Understand the dimensional threat

Scalable Resolution

Success level determines outcome: seal the rift locally, or discover this is part of a galactic invasion requiring campaign-scale response.
