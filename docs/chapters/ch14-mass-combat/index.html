Mass Combat Basics

Core Mass Combat Mechanic

2d10 + Commander Presence + Command + Unit Quality + Tags vs TN

Strategic Rounds: 1 hour of real time per round

Unit Actions: Each unit can move and attack once per round

Commander Actions: Limited to 1-3 special actions per round based on skill

Unit Statistics

All military units use the same stat block format:

Statistic Range Description
Size Squad (10) / Company (100) / Battalion (1000) / Division (10,000) Number of individuals or ships
Quality Green (0) / Regular (+1) / Veteran (+2) / Elite (+3) Training and experience level
Morale 3-15 (like health for units) Unit cohesion and fighting spirit
Equipment Poor (0) / Standard (+1) / Advanced (+2) / Cutting-Edge (+3) Gear and technology level
Tags 3-5 tags per unit [Unit type], [Doctrine], [Special abilities]

Time Scales

Scale Duration Use Case

Combined Arms Synergies

Units fighting together can provide bonuses:

Combination Bonus Requirements
Air Support +2 dice to ground attack Fighters + Ground units in same hex
Artillery Barrage +1 die, enemy -1 die Artillery within range of target
Combined Fleet +1 die per unit type Fighters, Corvettes, Capital ships together
Elite Spearhead +3 dice first round Elite unit leads attack

Battle Resolution

Battle Phases

  1. Strategic Phase (1 hour): Positioning, reconnaissance, initial maneuvers
  2. Engagement Phase (2-4 hours): Main combat with multiple strategic rounds
  3. Resolution Phase (1 hour): Pursuit, withdrawal, or occupation

Command Actions

Action Effect TN Duration
Rally Restore 1d6 Morale to one unit 11 1 action
Maneuver Reposition units, gain advantage 14 1 round
Focused Fire +2 damage to one target this round 14 1 action
Inspiring Speech All units +1 die next round 17 1 action
Tactical Genius Gain one extra command action 20 Special
Strategic Withdrawal Organized retreat with minimal losses 17 1 round

Combat Resolution

Initiative: 2d10 + Commander Awareness + Command + Fleet/Army tags

Attack: 2d10 + Unit Quality + Equipment + Situational tags vs enemy Defense TN

Defense TN: 10 + Target Unit Quality + Equipment + Defensive tags

Damage Resolution

Attack Success Morale Damage Additional Effect
Success by 1-3 1 point Unit shaken (loses next action)
Success by 4-6 2 points 5% casualties, -1 Quality until rally
Success by 7-9 3 points 10% casualties, -2 to all rolls
Success by 10+ 4 points 25% casualties, must make morale check

Victory Conditions

Condition Victory Points Effect
Rout Enemy Unit 1 VP Unit flees battlefield
Destroy Enemy Unit 2 VP Unit eliminated
Control Strategic Objective 3 VP Control key terrain/station
Break Enemy Army Morale 5 VP Mass retreat begins
Achieve Strategic Goal 10 VP Campaign objective completed

Winning: First side to 15 VP or control all objectives wins the battle.

PC Integration

Hero Actions

PCs can take special actions within mass combat that affect the larger battle:

Action Scale TN Effect
Duel Enemy Champion Personal Enemy's skill +5 Win = +2 army morale, lose = -1 morale
Sabotage Key Asset Squad 17 Enemy unit -2 dice next round
Rally Broken Unit Personal 14 Restore routed unit to 1 Morale
Assassination Personal 20 Remove enemy commander from battle
Hold the Line Squad 17 One unit becomes unbreakable for 3 rounds
Tactical Strike Squadron 14 Deal morale damage as if successful attack

Command Roles

Supreme Commander

  • Can use all command actions
  • Gets 3 actions per strategic round
  • Can coordinate multiple theaters
  • Presence affects entire army morale

Unit Commander

  • Affects only assigned units
  • Gets 1-2 actions per round
  • Can take personal hero actions
  • Adds skills to unit performance

Scaling Between Combat Types

Transitioning from mass combat to personal combat:

  • Boarding Actions: Personal combat during space battles
  • Commando Raids: Squad missions affecting army outcomes
  • Duels of Honor: Single combat with mass morale effects
  • Critical Objectives: Personal missions determining battle victory

Time Compression: Personal rounds (6 seconds) can occur within strategic rounds (1 hour) for dramatic moments.
