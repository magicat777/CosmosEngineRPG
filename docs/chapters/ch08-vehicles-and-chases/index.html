Chapter 8: Vehicles and Chases

Vehicles in Cosmos Engine are more than transportation—they're characters in their own right, with tags that define their personality and capabilities. From groundcars racing through neon-lit cities to starfighters dancing between asteroids, the Universal Tag System creates dynamic, narrative-driven vehicle encounters that scale from personal racing to fleet operations.

8.1 Vehicle Tag System

Understanding Vehicle Tags

Every vehicle has tags that describe its capabilities, limitations, and personality. These tags interact with pilot abilities and environmental conditions to create emergent gameplay.

Performance Tags

  • [Fast]: High top speed, acceleration
  • [Agile]: Superior maneuverability
  • [Stable]: Excellent control, hard to crash
  • [Durable]: Can take damage and keep going
  • [Efficient]: Extended range, low fuel consumption

Design Tags

  • [Sleek]: Aerodynamic design, stylish
  • [Rugged]: Built for harsh conditions
  • [Luxury]: Comfort and status features
  • [Military]: Combat-optimized systems
  • [Stealth]: Low sensor signature

Capability Tags

  • [Armed]: Weapon systems installed
  • [Shielded]: Energy defense systems
  • [Jump-Capable]: FTL travel ability
  • [Atmospheric]: Can operate in atmosphere
  • [Amphibious]: Water and land operation

Personality Tags

  • [Temperamental]: Quirky, requires special handling
  • [Loyal]: Responds best to bonded pilot
  • [Proud]: Performs better when respected
  • [Haunted]: Previous pilot's influence remains
  • [Awakened]: True AI consciousness

Vehicle-Pilot Synergies

When pilot and vehicle tags align, extraordinary performance emerges:

Speed Demon

Pilot: [Daredevil] [Fast] [Reckless]

Vehicle: [Fast] [Agile] [Temperamental]

Result: +3 dice to speed-based maneuvers

Precision Driver

Pilot: [Precise] [Calculating] [Patient]

Vehicle: [Stable] [Responsive] [Reliable]

Result: +2 dice, cannot fumble driving rolls

8.4 Chase Mechanics

The Chase Structure

Chases in Cosmos Engine use a combination of narrative description and mechanical resolution, with tags determining the flow of action.

Chase Phases

Opening

Establish starting positions, goals, and terrain. Determine who has advantage.

Pursuit

Series of contested rolls with complications and opportunities.

Resolution

Final dramatic moment that determines chase outcome.

Chase Complications

Each round, failed rolls or GM decisions introduce complications:

Traffic/Obstacles

  • Civilian vehicles block the path
  • Construction zones force detours
  • Emergency responders converge
  • Weather conditions worsen

Vehicle Problems

  • Engine overheating from stress
  • Tire/repulsor failure
  • Fuel/power running low
  • Systems malfunction

Environmental

  • Dangerous terrain ahead
  • Sudden gravity changes
  • Electromagnetic interference
  • Hostile wildlife appears

Third Parties

  • Additional pursuers join
  • Authority forces intervene
  • Rival gang interferes
  • Innocent bystanders in danger

Example Chase: Asteroid Mining Station

The Setup

Corporate security pursues smugglers through an active mining operation. Low gravity and industrial hazards create a three-dimensional chase through spinning machinery and plasma cutters.

Participants:

Smuggler: [Fast] [Agile] hoverbike, pilot with [Daredevil] [Lucky] tags

Security: [Armed] [Durable] patrol craft, pilot with [Military] [Precise] tags

Round 1: The Opening

Smuggler uses [Agile] + [Daredevil] to thread between plasma cutters (+2 dice). Security craft's [Armed] tag allows shooting while pursuing.

Round 2: Complication

Failed roll triggers "Mining Equipment Activation" - massive ore processor begins rotating, creating moving obstacles. Smuggler's [Lucky] tag helps avoid being crushed.

Resolution

Smuggler attempts desperate leap through closing blast doors. [Fast] + [Agile] + [Daredevil] + [Lucky] = +4 dice for this legendary maneuver!

8.5 Vehicle Combat

Combat Between Vehicles

Vehicle combat combines piloting skill, weapon systems, and tactical positioning. Tags determine both vulnerability and advantage.

Key Combat Factors

Speed vs Accuracy

[Fast] vehicles are harder to hit but have difficulty targeting. [Stable] vehicles can aim better but are easier targets.

Armor vs Maneuverability

[Durable] vehicles can absorb damage but are less agile. [Agile] craft avoid hits but are fragile when struck.

Range vs Environment

[Long-Range] weapons dominate open space. [Close-Combat] weapons excel in asteroids and city environments.

Pilot Skill Integration

Pilot tags combine with vehicle capabilities. [Tactical] pilots maximize [Armed] vehicle potential.

Vehicle Damage and Consequences

Damage Tracks

Vehicles use condition tracks rather than hit points:

Light Damage
  • Minor system glitches
  • Cosmetic damage
  • -1 to one type of roll
Moderate Damage
  • System failures
  • Performance degradation
  • -2 to affected systems
Critical Damage
  • Major system failure
  • Dangerous conditions
  • Risk of total shutdown
