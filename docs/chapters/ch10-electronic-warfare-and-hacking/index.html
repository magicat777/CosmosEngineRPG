Hacking Fundamentals

Core Hacking Mechanic

2d10 + Intellect + Technology + Equipment + Tags vs System TN

Required Equipment: Hacking deck or neural interface

Retry Rules: Each failure increases system TN by +2 until system resets (1-24 hours)

Ice & Countermeasures

Ice (Intrusion Countermeasure Electronics) are defensive programs that actively resist hackers:

Ice Type Security Level Effect Defeat Method
Barrier Basic-Commercial Blocks access, no damage Overcome with successful hack
Trace Commercial-Secure Identifies hacker location Break connection quickly
Feedback Secure-Military 1d6 neural damage on failure [Shielded] equipment protects
Hunter Military Pursues hacker through network Destroy at source or outrun
Killer Military-AI 2d6 neural damage, system lockout Military-grade equipment required
Guardian AI AI Core Intelligent opposition, adapts tactics Negotiate, trick, or overwhelming force

Failure Consequences

Failed By Immediate Consequence Long-term Effect
1-4 Access denied, can retry System TN +2 for 1 hour
5-9 System alerts, security increases All TNs +2, admin notified
10-14 Traced, countermeasures activate Ice programs attack, location known
15+ Burned, permanent lockout + damage Equipment damaged, legal/physical consequences

Extended Tests

Complex systems require multiple successes:

System Complexity Successes Required Examples
Simple 1 Personal devices, basic terminals
Standard 3 Corporate systems, ship computers
Complex 5 Government networks, military bases
Massive 10 Planetary networks, AI consciousness

Extended Test Rules:

  • Each attempt takes the base time for the action
  • Failure consequences apply on each failed roll
  • Team members can contribute (see Team Hacking below)

Team Hacking

Multiple hackers can coordinate attacks:

Roles

  • Lead: Makes primary rolls, directs team
  • Support: +1 die per helper (max +3)
  • Overwatch: Monitors for security responses
  • Specialist: Handles specific system types

Combat Hacking

  • Quick Access: 1 action, TN +4
  • Emergency Override: 1 action, TN +6
  • System Crash: 1 action, TN +8
  • Shutdown: 1 action, damages equipment

Avatar Combat Mechanics

In full VR environments, hackers appear as avatars and can engage in digital combat:

  • Avatar Health: Equal to character's Resolve + Technology
  • Digital Weapons: Damage = program rating + Intellect
  • Virtual Armor: Defense programs provide DR
  • Environment Effects: AI can reshape battlefields

Avatar Damage Effects

Light:Visual distortion, -1 to actions
Moderate:Sensory feedback, -2 to actions
Critical:Neural shock, 1d6 real damage
Destroyed:Forced disconnection, 2d6 real damage

AI Encounters

True AIs inhabit digital space as their natural environment. Confronting them requires exceptional tactics:

AI Advantages

  • Home field advantage (+3 dice in own systems)
  • Can reshape virtual environment at will
  • Process information at superhuman speeds
  • Multiple simultaneous conversations
  • Perfect memory and instant data access

AI Limitations

  • May not understand human emotions
  • Logical thinking patterns are predictable
  • Often bound by original programming
  • Can be distracted by interesting puzzles
  • May have built-in ethical constraints

Negotiation with AIs: Often more effective than brute force. Use Presence + skills, with bonus dice for [Logical], [Curious], or [Empathetic] tags.
