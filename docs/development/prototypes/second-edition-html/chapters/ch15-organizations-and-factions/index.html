Chapter 15: Organizations and Factions

The galaxy pulses with the ambitions of countless organizations, each defined by their own tags and driven by distinct agendas. Understanding these factions—and how character tags interact with their goals—creates rich opportunities for alliance, conflict, and political intrigue.

15.1 Faction Tag System

Every organization has tags that define their methods, goals, and culture. These tags influence how they react to PCs and what opportunities they provide.

Organizational Tags

Structure Tags

  • [Hierarchical]: Clear chain of command
  • [Democratic]: Collective decision making
  • [Corporate]: Profit-driven structure
  • [Secretive]: Hidden operations and goals

Philosophy Tags

  • [Progressive]: Embraces change and innovation
  • [Traditional]: Preserves established ways
  • [Militant]: Solves problems through force
  • [Diplomatic]: Prefers negotiation

15.2 Major Galactic Powers

The Terran Federation

Tags: [Democratic], [Expansionist], [Diverse], [Idealistic], [Bureaucratic]

Goals: Spread human values and democracy throughout the galaxy

Resources: Advanced technology, large population, strong economy

PC Opportunities: Diplomatic missions, exploration contracts, military service

Potential Conflicts: Bureaucratic red tape, cultural imperialism, resource competition

Hyperion Industries

Tags: [Corporate], [Ruthless], [Innovative], [Wealthy], [Amoral]

Goals: Maximum profit and market dominance across all sectors

Resources: Unlimited funding, cutting-edge technology, corporate espionage

PC Opportunities: High-paying contracts, prototype equipment, corporate security

Potential Conflicts: Unethical experiments, worker exploitation, environmental destruction

The Order of Cosmic Harmony

Tags: [Spiritual], [Ancient], [Wise], [Pacifist], [Mysterious]

Goals: Maintain galactic peace and preserve cosmic knowledge

Resources: Ancient wisdom, psychic abilities, hidden sanctuaries

PC Opportunities: Spiritual training, ancient knowledge, peaceful mediation

Potential Conflicts: Rigid philosophy, opposition to violence, hidden agendas

15.3 Faction Relationships

PC reputation with organizations is tracked through relationship tags that evolve based on actions:

Positive Relationships

  • [Trusted Agent]: High-level access and missions
  • [Valued Ally]: Mutual support and resources
  • [Honorary Member]: Recognition and respect
  • [Protected Asset]: Organization provides security

Negative Relationships

  • [Known Threat]: Active opposition and hindrance
  • [Marked Target]: Assassination attempts
  • [Persona Non Grata]: Banned from territories
  • [Ideological Enemy]: Fundamental opposition

15.4 Organizational Evolution

Factions change based on PC actions and galactic events. Success and failure reshape their tags and capabilities:

Example: Federation Response to Crisis

Initial Tags: [Democratic], [Idealistic], [Bureaucratic]

Crisis: Massive alien invasion threatens human space

PC Actions: Lead successful defense, inspire unity

New Tags: [Democratic], [Idealistic], [Military-Focused], [PC-Grateful]

Result: Federation becomes more militarized but maintains core values

15.5 Creating Custom Factions

Faction Design Framework

  1. Core Purpose: What does this organization exist to do?
  2. Key Tags: Choose 3-5 tags that define their approach
  3. Resources: What advantages and limitations do they have?
  4. Conflicts: Who or what opposes them?
  5. PC Hooks: How can characters interact with them?

Tag Interaction Guidelines

  • Matching tags between PCs and factions create opportunities
  • Opposing tags generate conflict and tension
  • Complementary tags suggest mutually beneficial relationships
  • Evolving tags reflect how PC actions change organizations
