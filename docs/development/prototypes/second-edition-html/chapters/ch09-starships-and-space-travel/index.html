9.1 Starship Tag System

Understanding Starship Tags

Starships possess tags that define their capabilities, design philosophy, and operational characteristics. These tags interact with crew abilities and environmental conditions to create unique adventures.

Size & Scale Tags

  • [Fighter]: Single-pilot craft, maximum agility
  • [Corvette]: Small crew, fast and maneuverable
  • [Frigate]: Multi-role vessel, balanced capabilities
  • [Cruiser]: Heavy firepower, extended operations
  • [Dreadnought]: Massive, fleet-commanding presence

Design Philosophy Tags

  • [Military]: Combat-optimized systems
  • [Civilian]: Comfort and efficiency focused
  • [Explorer]: Long-range scientific mission
  • [Trader]: Cargo capacity and economy
  • [Smuggler]: Hidden compartments, stealth systems

Performance Tags

  • [Fast]: Superior speed and acceleration
  • [Agile]: Excellent maneuverability
  • [Durable]: Heavy armor and redundant systems
  • [Stealthy]: Low sensor signature
  • [Long-Range]: Extended operational capability

Personality Tags

  • [Proud]: Ship has reputation to maintain
  • [Loyal]: Bonds strongly with crew
  • [Haunted]: Previous crew's influence remains
  • [Lucky]: Survives impossible situations
  • [Cursed]: Attracts trouble and misfortune

Ship-Crew Synergies

When crew abilities align with ship characteristics, extraordinary performance emerges:

Perfect Harmony

Captain: [Inspiring] [Tactical] [Leader]

Ship: [Military] [Loyal] [Proven]

Result: +3 dice to all ship operations

Explorer's Bond

Pilot: [Curious] [Brave] [Adaptive]

Ship: [Explorer] [Durable] [Lucky]

Result: Discover hidden routes and phenomena

9.2 Starship Economy and Ownership

Starship Wealth Categories

Starships represent the highest tier of investment in Cosmos Engine. Most characters will never personally own a starship, instead serving as crew or gaining access through organizations.

Ship Category Credit Range Typical Access Examples
Fighter Class 5M-50M cr Military/Corporate pilots Interceptors, scouts
Corvette Class 50M-200M cr Small corporations, crews Patrol ships, runners
Frigate Class 200M-1B cr Major corporations Traders, explorers
Capital Ships 1B-50B cr Governments, mega-corps Cruisers, dreadnoughts

Starship Access Methods

Crew Positions
  • Hired crew on existing ships
  • Profit-sharing arrangements
  • Work toward partial ownership
  • Build reputation for better positions
Organizational Ships
  • Military service assignments
  • Corporate fleet access
  • Patron-provided vessels
  • Mission-specific loans
Alternative Acquisition
  • Salvage rights on derelicts
  • Prize ships from combat
  • Inheritance or gifts
  • Black market deals
Financing Options
  • Bank loans (30% down minimum)
  • Investor groups
  • Criminal syndicate backing
  • Government subsidies

Operating Costs

Owning a starship involves significant ongoing expenses beyond the initial purchase:

9.4 Faster-Than-Light Travel

Jump Drive Technology

FTL travel in Cosmos Engine uses quantum tunneling through hyperspace, with journey times depending on distance, ship capabilities, and navigator skill.

Jump Classification

System Jumps (1-5 light years)
  • Duration: Hours to days
  • Risk: Minimal
  • Navigation: Computer autopilot
  • Tags: [Routine], [Safe], [Predictable]
Sector Jumps (5-50 light years)
  • Duration: Days to weeks
  • Risk: Low
  • Navigation: Skilled pilot recommended
  • Tags: [Standard], [Commercial], [Mapped]
Long Jumps (50-500 light years)
  • Duration: Weeks to months
  • Risk: Moderate
  • Navigation: Expert navigator required
  • Tags: [Challenging], [Dangerous], [Remote]
Deep Jumps (500+ light years)
  • Duration: Months to years
  • Risk: High
  • Navigation: Master navigator essential
  • Tags: [Legendary], [Uncharted], [Perilous]

Jump Hazards and Complications

Hyperspace travel isn't without dangers. Failed navigation rolls or environmental hazards can create complications:

Navigation Errors

  • Emergence at wrong location
  • Extended travel time
  • Jump drive stress/damage
  • Lost in uncharted space

Hyperspace Phenomena

  • Quantum storms disrupt navigation
  • Dimensional rifts alter course
  • Time dilation effects
  • Encounters with interdimensional entities

Emergence Complications

  • Hostile forces waiting
  • Stellar phenomena interference
  • Emergency system repairs needed
  • Crew disorientation/sickness

Exotic Encounters

  • Ancient hyperspace beacons
  • Derelict ships adrift in jump space
  • Messages from alternate timelines
  • Hyperspace-dwelling creatures

9.5 Space Combat

Space Combat Fundamentals

Combat in space operates on a vastly different scale than personal or vehicle combat. Distances are measured in thousands of kilometers, and engagements can last hours or even days.

Combat Range Bands

Close Range (1-100 km)
  • All weapon systems effective
  • Ramming attacks possible
  • Detailed targeting available
  • Tags: [Point-Blank], [Desperate], [Personal]
Short Range (100-1,000 km)
  • Standard combat engagement
  • Most weapons effective
  • Maneuvering room limited
  • Tags: [Standard], [Tactical], [Engaged]
Medium Range (1,000-10,000 km)
  • Long-range weapons preferred
  • Time delay affects targeting
  • Room for complex maneuvers
  • Tags: [Distant], [Strategic], [Methodical]
Long Range (10,000+ km)
  • Only heavy weapons effective
  • Sensor warfare important
  • Escape routes available
  • Tags: [Remote], [Cautious], [Probing]

Starship Weapons

9.6 Legendary Starships

Ships of Legend

Some starships transcend their original design, becoming legends through their deeds, their crews, or the forces that shaped them.

The Eternal Wanderer

Class: Ancient Explorer Vessel

Tags: [Legendary], [Ancient], [Self-Repairing], [Sentient], [Wise], [Protective], [Home]

History: This ship has been exploring the galaxy for over a thousand years. Its original builders are long forgotten, but the ship continues its mission, choosing its own crews from among those who share its wanderlust.

Special Abilities:

  • Repairs itself using any available matter
  • Knows safe routes through dangerous space
  • Can survive in environments that would destroy other ships
  • Protects its crew like family
  • Contains libraries of knowledge from a hundred civilizations

Quirks: The ship sometimes changes course on its own to investigate interesting phenomena. It has strong opinions about crew behavior and may refuse to cooperate if mistreated.

The Void Reaper

Class: Cursed Dreadnought

Tags: [Dreadnought], [Cursed], [Deadly], [Haunted], [Unstoppable], [Feared], [Doomed]

History: Built during the War of Shadows, this ship was cursed by the dying breath of a billion souls. Every crew eventually meets a tragic end, but not before achieving impossible victories.

Special Abilities:

  • Weapons are unnaturally accurate and deadly
  • Enemy ships suffer system failures in its presence
  • Can phase partially out of reality to avoid damage
  • Crew gains supernatural combat abilities
  • Never truly destroyed—always returns eventually

Curse: Every crew member will die heroically but tragically. The ship ensures they achieve legendary status before their inevitable doom.

9.7 Quick Reference

Starship Operations Summary

Ship Scale Progression

  • Fighter: Personal craft (1 pilot)
  • Corvette: Small crew (6-12)
  • Frigate: Medium crew (20-50)
  • Cruiser: Large crew (200-800)
  • Dreadnought: Massive crew (2000+)

Jump Distance Guidelines

  • System: 1-5 LY (Hours-Days)
  • Sector: 5-50 LY (Days-Weeks)
  • Long: 50-500 LY (Weeks-Months)
  • Deep: 500+ LY (Months-Years)

Medium 1,000-10,000 km -1 +1
